About

The New Born Stabilizing Units (NBSU's) Portal was created to make it easy for parents, medical professionals and statisticians to review and analyze District Wise Data related to infants who were admitted to NBSU's. Newborns Weighing 1800 - 2500 gms are admitted at NBSU's where they are provide with resuscitation facilities, warmth and are protected from infections. Sick babies are diagnosed, referred to a suitable doctors and then discharged. Care is provided: At Birth,Resuscitation, provision of warmth, prevention of infection, early initiation of breastfeeding and weighing the newborn are the processes carried out In case of a Normal Newborn,Breastfeeding/ feeding support is provided. If the newborn is diagnosed sick,Management of low birth weight infants ⩾1800 g with no other complication, phototherapy for newborns with hyper-bilirubinemia, management of newborn sepsis, stabilization and referral of sick newborns and those with very low birth weight (rooming in) and referral services are provided.





Benefits:


1. .NBSUs expand the reach of special newborn care within a district, beyond the district hospital

2. Reduces delay in initiation of appropriate care for sick newborns and improves outcomes

3. Helps in stabilizing sick newborns before referral to higher centre

4. Prevent overloading of SNCUs at the district hospital

5. Reduce the cost of care
